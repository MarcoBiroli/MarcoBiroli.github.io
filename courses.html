Physics

This is a course taught by Pr. Adel Bilal in the Master ICFP program that I am currently following. We introduced relativistic quantum mechanics by taking a semi-historical approach. We started from the postulates of both theories to develop the Klein- Gordon Equation. After noticing the failures of the Klein-Gordon equation we used Lie groups/algebras symmetry arguments to derive the Dirac equation. We are currently introducing Quantum Field Theory.

This is a course taught by Pr. Nick Kaiser in the Master ICFP program that I am currently following. We started by reviewing special relativity and developping the concepts of differential geometry in curved space-time and derived the Einstein field equations. We then developped the weak-field theory of gravity and applied it to gravitational waves and discussed other applications including black-holes and elementary stellar structure theory ; gravitational lensing ; the solar system (precession of Mercury’s orbit) ; and the derivation of the singularity theorems from Raychaudhuri’s equation. The second half of the course is deidcated to cosmology and is still ongoing.

This is a course taught by Pr. Stephane Fauve in the Master ICFP program that I am currently following. The first part of the semester concerned deterministic dynamical systems. Specifically the study of non-linear systems, their equilibriums/bifurcations. We also saw the universality of the behaviors close to bifurcations and how a series of bifurcations can lead to chaos.

This is a course taught by Pr. Frederic Chevy in the Master ICFP program that I followed. The course gave us the mathematical formalism and tools to study symmetries in physics. We started from the bases of group theory, then moved to the fundamentals of topology in order to introduce Lie groups and Lie algebras properly.

This is a course taught by Pr. Nathan Goldman in the Master ICFP program that I followed. We introduced some general concets of differential geometry, mainly: manifolds, fiber bundles, Chern numbers, Berry connection/curvature/phase. We then studied their physical application and meaning in different contexts ranging from superfluid vortices to the Haldane model. Finally we introduced the mathematics that justify the study of artificial matter using Flouquet engineering.

Mathematics

This is a course taught by Pr. Raphael Cerf in the Master ICFP program that I followed. We started from the formalism behind modern probability theory and a quick overview of the important measure theory theorems without always proving them. We then moved on to random walks, the law of large numbers, the central limit theorem and finished with percolation.

This is a Bachelor 3 course of the ENS math department taught by Pr. Nicolas Bergeron. We started from the basics of complex analyis: definitions of holomorphic functions, Cauchy's Theorem, Goursat's Lemma. We then saw the main theorems of complex analysis: Liouville's theorem, zeros of analytic functions, Shwartz's Lemma, integral of holomorphic functions, Weierstrass products and so on. We then studied meromorphic functions and residues. The second half of the course focused on more in-depth topics such as: indices, conformal representations, coverings, modular functions, harmonic functions and elliptic modular functions.
