Internships

2021 Second Semester Internship with Satya Majumdar at Orsay - LPTMS on resetting random walks

Resetting random walks emerged 10 years ago as a seemingly simple modification of usual random walkers. We simply add a new behavior: the walker is allowed to jump back to the origin with a certain probability. This seemingly simple modification leads to the emergence of very different behaviors and allows us to model very different phenomena. During my internship I derived the distribution of the number of visited sites for a random walker with Poissonian resetting. The paper is currently in writing.

2020 Summer Internship with Werner Krauth at ENS on Event Chain Monte Carlo algorithms

Event Chain Monte Carlo algorithms are current state of the art algorithms used to simulate complex many-bodies interacting systems. In my internship I focused primarily on the extension of such algorithms so that they can be used on non-spherical particles so as to study phenomena such as phase transitions in liquid crystals. By the end of the two month internship I managed to develop an algorithm that works with solid boundary conditions. We are currently developing the extension to periodic boundary conditions.

2019 Summer Internship with Laurent Sanchez Palencia at CPHT on Quantum localization transitions in quasi-periodic lattice systems

In collaboration with Alessandro Pacco we worked for two months on studying the fractal properties of quantum particles in quasi-periodic potentials. We contributed to the numerical tools built by the Quantum Matter group and used them to study the fractal dimensions at critical values of a quasi-periodic potential.

2018 Summer Internship with Adina Panchea on Robot Path/Motion Planning.

During this two month internship I developped a new Path/Motion planning algorithm based on rapidly exploring random trees. I also coded a graphical interface regrouping all most commonly known tree based path/motion planning algorithms.
